“New business, more jobs” in Charters Towers

The Poppet Head Plaza Wall of History at Goldtower Central

Originally published for the North Queensland newspaper, Sumpton's Gold Rush Gazette.

Executive Director of Goltower Central, Paul McIver has predicted big things for Charters Towers as his commercial precinct continues to score big names.

The rapidly approaching opening of Pets Domain at Goldtower Central on October 7 has sparked encouraging words from Mr McIver about the future of Charters Towers.

Mr McIver said locals can look forward to a boom in business.

"It looks like we have fully leased Treasure Towers to four national branded tenants,” he said

“These tenants will bring new business, more jobs and of course an increased Charters Towers shopping experience for our town and region."

The opening of Pets Domain at Goldtower Central will coincide with the ‘Pawsome Day Out’ open day filled with pet related activities.

A spokeswoman for Pets Domain said “Supporting regional Australian pet parents,” was a core focus for the business.

The predicted business boom for Charters Towers forecasts big things for the local economy.
